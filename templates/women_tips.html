Safety Tips

Stay informed, stay safe

Safe Cab Travel

Essential tips for ride-sharing
Always verify the driver's photo and vehicle number before getting in
Share your live trip with a trusted contact
Sit in the back seat behind the driver
Keep your phone charged and data on

Public Transport Safety

Bus, metro & train tips
Stand near the driver or in well-lit areas
Use women's coaches in metro when available
Keep valuables in front pockets
Avoid isolated bus stops late at night

Walking Alone

Safety while walking
Stay on well-lit, busy streets
Avoid using headphones in unfamiliar areas
Walk confidently and be aware of surroundings
Keep your phone easily accessible

Home Entry Safety

Before opening the door
Always use peephole or video doorbell
Verify delivery persons with the company
Don't share that you live alone

Home Security

Keep your home safe
Install good quality locks on all doors
Keep emergency numbers on speed dial
Know your neighbors and share contacts

Online Privacy

Protect your data
Avoid sharing live locations on social media
Use strong, unique passwords
Be cautious with friend requests from strangers

Cyber Harassment

Dealing with online abuse
Screenshot and save all evidence
Block and report the harasser
File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in

Emergency Numbers

Important helplines
112 - Emergency Response
181 - Women Helpline
1091 - Women in Distress
100 - Police

If You Feel Unsafe

Immediate actions
Make noise and attract attention
Move towards crowded areas or shops
Press SOS button or call 112
Enter a public place and ask for help

Know Your Rights

Legal protections
Police must register your FIR
You can file Zero FIR at any police station
Female officer must be present for women's statement
You cannot be arrested between sunset and sunrise

Self Defense Basics

Protect yourself
Target sensitive areas: eyes, nose, throat, groin
Use everyday items: keys, umbrella, bag
Yell "FIRE" instead of "Help" for more response
Consider taking a self-defense class
